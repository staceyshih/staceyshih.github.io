Shared TW - Donation Platform

Overview

Inspired by the donation surge during Taiwan's 2021 COVID outbreak, Shared TW explores how to better match material donations with real-time needs. When disasters strike, supply imbalances often arise — some places run short while others receive excess. This project aimed to build a lightweight platform that could transparently match demand and donation in a timely, decentralized way.

Goal

Enable organizations in need and individuals who wish to donate to efficiently connect, reducing communication friction and resource waste.

Role
UI/UX Design
Timeline

Research

Scattered Information

Interviews with hospital social workers revealed that many local organizations post needs only on their own social media, limiting visibility.

Minimizing Intermediaries

Existing platforms often rely on institutional intermediaries. Shared TW was designed to facilitate direct matching, skipping payment and logistics layers in its MVP for faster deployment.

Due to the unstable pandemic situation, it was necessary to complete the first phase of design as quickly as possible.

Design Strategies

Created a basic member system with two user roles: donor and requester.

Donors can browse needs by organization, region, or type and submit donation offers.

Requesters can post needs, manage submissions, and update fulfillment status.

Solutions

Donation Process

Donation decision-making

Donors can check if specific organizations have relevant needs and filter by region or type. To donate, they fill in quantity and delivery time. For simplicity, needs are shown by organization.

Ps. After discussions with the backend engineer, we temporarily display needs based on the requesting organization to reduce data complexity.

Donation tracking

After applying, donors can track status, update or cancel requests before approval. Once approved, they get contact info to coordinate directly.

Request Process

Post a Request

After logging in, recipient organizations can create a request by entering the item name and required quantity. If the need is complex, additional details can be provided in the notes section.

Ps. Organizations must verify their accounts using an official email address. If the email domain is not from a verified organization, registration will be denied.

Request Management

On the organization member page, there are sections for "Needs List," "Application List," and "Notification Center," allowing frontline teams to edit requests at any time and track the fulfillment rate.

Learning & Next Step

While we completed the MVP and engaged with hospital units, interviews revealed a key blocker: privacy concerns. Many organizations prefer to share needs through trusted networks rather than public platforms, to avoid exposing sensitive information.

As a grassroots team, we faced adoption challenges. However, the platform framework remains valuable for future use in verified or closed-network scenarios.
