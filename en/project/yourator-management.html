Application Management Redesign

Overview

Refine the user flow of Application Management, allowing mobile users to easily check the resume and send messages.

Achieved a 62% improvement in HR’s average response time. Enhanced both the candidate experience and employer satisfaction.

Project Goal

Empowered HR teams to effortlessly manage resumes and respond to candidates across ALL devices, reducing average response time and elevating the overall hiring experience.

Timeline
2021 SEP. - 2021 DEC.
Role
User Behavior Research
UI/UX Design
Team Size
2 Product Designers

Background

In 2017, when building the backend system, we found that most HR professionals managed recruitment tasks mainly on desktops. Considering development costs and user scenarios, the mobile version was designed just for displaying information.

As user habits shifted, more HR professionals wanted mobile management. Fast-growing startups using Yourator also needed faster workflows, but the old backend couldn’t keep up, leading to many manual support requests and low efficiency.

**The team decided to optimize the backend, collecting around 60 survey responses. Improvements focused on core "recruitment management" features, especially "filtering" and "internal messaging," where users struggled most.**

Discovery

Due to the availability of long-term behavior tracking tools (e.g., Hotjar, Google Analytics), a large volume of user complaints and feature requests from the sales team, and time constraints, we ultimately selected the following 2 research methods to help the team identify and prioritize key problems:

1. User Behavior Analysis

Observed click maps, mouse heatmaps, and reviewed 60+ user session recordings from the past six months related to the application management flow.

2. Internal Expert Interview

Interviewed 2 members from each of the following roles to identify common pain points when using the backend system.

  1. Headhunters - Who are familiar with Hiring process.
  2. Business Developers/Account Managers - Who are familiar with Users' common issues.

Insights

1. Users need to view all related information while replying to messages.

In the heatmap and screen recording observations, many users "click on View Full Resume button immediately" after opening the message.

During internal interviews, headhunters also mentioned: "In order to provide a good candidate experience, they always cross-check information before and after communication to avoid errors."

2. Resume Viewing and Message Reply Should Be More Integrated

In the previous design, the "View Resume" and "Send Message" buttons were separated. Also, the system required HR to "view the resume first" before initiating a conversation with the candidate.

However, during later interviews and feedback, it was discovered that this requirement didn’t fit all use cases and actually increased frustration and confusion.

3. Diverse Device Types Used

HR professionals primarily use company-provided office computers, which results in a greater variety of device types, without a specific pattern.

Through Google Analytics, it was found that screen widths ranging from 1000px to 2480px were being used. The most common screen widths used were 1920px, 1440px, 1280px, and 420px.

Design Strategies

Integrate candidates' information, Unify the feature entrances, Support display on various devices.

HRs are experts of Multi-tasking, they must integrate multiple types of information to make comprehensive judgments.
To help HR manage recruitment progress more efficiently, we reorganized the backend page structure, enhanced status indicators to make items that need attention more visible, relaxed system restrictions, and added many shortcuts, making recruitment management more aligned with user needs.

1. Unify the entrance of application management

All information related to a specific candidate is now consolidated on a single screen. On the same page level, HR can view resumes, message history, and reply using quick message templates.

2. Add Clear Message Status Hint

In previous version, the message entry was only represented by an icon, which was not clear enough.

In the new version, we took the HR perspective and provided clearer prompts with text such as "Open Conversation," "You Have Replied," "New Message," etc., guiding users to take the appropriate actions.

3. Added "Unread Messages" Entry on the Dashboard

HR can now instantly view key information such as "Applications," "Messages," and "Job Postings" as soon as they enter the backend Dashboard page, staying up-to-date with the latest recruitment activities. They can directly click to reply to unread messages.

4.Smooth Experience Across All Devices

In the new design, to ensure users on mobile, narrow screens, and wide screens can all have a smooth experience, we implemented expandable screen components.

Additional breakpoints and settings for minimum and maximum widths of components and containers were also configured. During the process, we communicated the UI changes more clearly to front-end developers through flexible plug-ins.

Outcome

Within one month of the launch of this project, HR's average "time to review submissions" decreased by 22%, from 2.4 days to 1.8 days.

With job posting and submission volumes staying consistent, this reflects a 62% boost in response speed, helping candidates receive feedback faster.
Operationally, customer complaints significantly decreased, freeing up the business team to focus on growth.

The backend upgrade also supported the launch of new pricing plans, driving additional revenuefor the company.

Reflections

Future Plans

  1. Bulk actions. When there's a huge amount of applications flooded in, HRs still need to process it 1 by 1 for now, which is frustrating somehow.
  2. Viewing history by account. It was hard to tell if this resume was read by which HR, and users need to memorize their own viewing history.
  3. Include note or labeling features to help HRs async information with cross-function hiring teams.

Learnings

There are in average 200 Funders and HRs would log in our service. We hope this update would not influence their hiring process.

Therefore, I planned the backend product adoption process, including satisfaction surveys, feature comparisons, updated service manuals, feedback channels, and fallback options.

Thanks to a smooth onboarding experience, no users switched back to the old version after one month, proving the redesign was intuitive and easy to adopt.

Since this project iterated on a design I contributed to as an intern in 2017, which allowed me to reflect on earlier design limitations and deliver a solution that truly met user needs.
