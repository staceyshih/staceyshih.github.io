Content Discovery Research on Big Screen

Overview

We gained deep insights into the browsing behavior of both new and experienced users on big screens, through cross-functional collaboration, precise user segmentation, and diverse research methods (surveys, interviews, high-fidelity prototype testing), which led to the following outcomes:

  1. Identified key behavioral triggers that lead to content viewing: provided crucial information that sparks user interest
  2. Influenced feature development and UX improvements for Q2–Q3 2024, such as: autoplaying trailers on content pages and simplifying information density
  3. Provided long-term insights on content strategy and design direction

Goal

Business Goal: Increase watch rate among new and existing users
Research Goal: How do users browse content on big screens? What motivates them to click and watch a video?

Role
  • User Research planning
  • User Interview
  • Prototype Design
Timeline
2023 OCT. - 2024 JAN.
Team Size
1 UX Designer(me) + 0.5 UI Designer

Background

At the beginning of 2022, the product team conducted a behavioral analysis on the web, categorizing users based on whether they performed "key actions" (saving to my list, watching, searching, clicking categories). Among users whose actions included key behaviors, an average of 79% proceeded to watch content. In contrast, users whose actions did not include key behaviors and who did not visit any item pages (internally defined as "pure browsing" users) had only 4% who proceeded to watch.



At the time, we tried to analyze the common behavioral paths of the "browsing-only" user group based on their action steps. However, due to the wide variety of paths, we couldn’t identify a dominant pattern that could justify a valid product design intervention. Therefore, we aimed to use qualitative research to directly observe and understand what factors influence "browsing-only" users to start watching content during their interaction. To balance research cost, we had to focus on one device type as the main topic. Considering the following two reasons, the team chose "TV" as the primary device for this study:

  1. TV users tend to have a higher watch rate and longer subscription duration
  2. The team hasn’t conducted any Big Screen-related user research in recent years

Research Plan

Before starting the study, we spent about two weeks working with the business analysis team, product managers, engineers, and editorial team to clarify research hypotheses. Considering research cost and effectiveness, we decided to conduct user interviews in the Taiwan market for this phase.

Target user segmentation

To reduce bias caused by familiarity with the app, we interviewed two user groups — six beginners and six experienced users aged 25–45, which aligns with CATCHPLAY’s target audience. Among all devices, TV was the most used for watching content.

Preliminary survey

We sent a preliminary questionnaire to existing users who matched the criteria, collecting both quantitative data and interview willingness. We received 180 valid responses. Data collected included: subjective rating of how difficult it is to find content on CP, importance of different video information types, and time spent using CP+.

Participant selection

Among willing participants, we selected based on:

  1. Watching TV over 1 hour per week
  2. Average difficulty in finding content
  3. Balanced gender and age distribution
  4. Preference given to those with experience renting single videos, based on usage data

Survey sharing

Participants selected common motivations for using OTT platforms, shared their brand perception, and listed familiar browsing-related features. This helped us gain a baseline understanding before interviews and served as an icebreaker to help participants focus.

User interviews

Participants were asked to recall the most recent video they watched on TV and replicate the browsing process using the actual TV. If they couldn’t recall, they were asked to search for a video of interest on the spot. During this, we asked follow-up questions to explore what information they valued and their typical behaviors.

Concept prototype – usability testing

We tested a high-fidelity prototype to validate these hypotheses:

  1. Which is more helpful: brief category info or teaser clip previews?
  2. Which navigation structure helps users find desired content more easily?
  3. Would a smoother transition from homepage to item page increase viewing intent?

These hypotheses were distilled from browsing-related design proposals made by team members in mid-2023. Some topics like brand impression were unsuitable for testing, so we used surveys instead. The goal was to make the study more aligned with internal ideas and provide clearer design directions.

Outcome

1. Users skim and sense, not read word-by-word
Users decide in 3–5 seconds whether to engage with a video and are sensitive to subtle differences.

Suggestions:

  1. Refine Quick View to reduce info overload.
  2. Replace long descriptions with concise tags like genre, director, cast, plot summary, and IMDb score.
2. Simpler navigation encourages discovery
Overly complex interfaces or too many choices hinder exploration.

Suggestion:

Streamline category browsing, avoid excessive subcategories.

3. Match content to emotional needs
New users often seek mood-shifting content with no specific goal; advanced users tend to search purposefully.

A user commented, “Your content is great, but I don’t always want something heavy,” showing that the current curation style may not suit daily viewing needs.

Suggestion: Balance deep, intense films with lighter viewing options.
4. Personalization boosts engagement
Users appreciate recommendations but want control.

Suggestions:

  1. Allow users to filter by preferred genres.
  2. Recommendations should be adjustable and easy to opt out from.

Records of the research process

1. 設計不同「瀏覽」方式，吸引使用者點擊

75% 受訪者喜好 自動播預告 的版本，因為視覺容易被動態吸引，且版面與操作更簡潔、影片內含的資訊量更大，利於感受＆判讀

2. 調整導覽列以符合使用者查找需求

以上兩種導覽方式各有使用者支持，但共同點為「導覽列不要太過複雜，或是選項過多」。

3. 簡化切換至影片頁面的操作流程

測試後，75% 受訪者偏好現有流程 - 透過首頁資訊判讀對影片的興趣，進入 影片內容頁 後「專注」於了解該影片，新版本對頁面的可掌握性較低，也無法讓使用者快速找到「導演演員」、「相關推薦」等資訊區塊。 不過，新提案也有一些優點，例如：自動播放預告片、資訊一目瞭然等，未來也可以納入調整方向。

訪談照片紀錄

訪談過程中，我們會以使用者正面的電視為主，並提供另一台電視，作為兩版本比較時的畫面輔助。

Learning & Next Step

高擬真原型的重要性

由於研究題目涉及探索內容的流程，無論是操作或內容層面，都需具備高度真實性與足夠的探索範圍，才能讓使用者自然融入情境並分享更多想法。 此次在製作研究素材時，參考了許多 Figma、Protopie、YouTube 的公開檔案與 Key Mapping 工具教學。

由於當時 Figma 尚未有可操作的原型，我也在本次研究完成後，將檔案整理後上傳分享到 Figma 社群 ，希望能回饋帶給我很多啟發的設計社群。

清楚地傳遞研究發現

研究完成後，在簡報分享時，也需要針對不同的聽眾提供對應的可行建議。起初為了避免過於明確而導致建議不夠具體，收到團隊回饋「意見過於模糊」，因此後來透過 UX 團隊腦力激盪，先提出幾項具體建議作為輔助。未來簡報前可先了解聽眾期待與聽完簡報後的下一步動作。

AI 輔助研究流程

本次研究改為使用 Good Tape AI 語音辨識工具，幫助將錄音檔轉換為逐字稿，準確率比以往高很多。雖然依舊需要人工聽打特定語句、品牌名稱等 部分。但光是發現並採用這個工具，協助團隊節省了 50% 的逐字稿整理時間。
